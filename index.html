Neighborhoods in Philadelphia

Center City  |  Old City  |  Fisthown  |  Queen Village

Center City

Center City is the is both the geographical and business heart of Philadelphia. It holds Philadelphia's city hall which houses the majority of Philadelphia's governmental offices including the Mayors office. It is also home to many skyscrapers which house many large businesses. There are some upscale restuarants and shops in this are, parks (including the famous Love Park), and even an ice skating rink in the winter!

Old City

Old City, Philadelphia is one of the most historic neighborhoods in the country! It is home to independance hall where the Declaration of Independance was signed by the Founding Fathers. It also houses the Liberty Bell, Penn's Landing (Where William Penn first landed when founding Philadelphia), Elfreth's Alley -- the oldest continous residential street in America, and many museums dedicated to the time period.

Fishtown

Fishtown was, historically, a working class residential area that has become a thriving center for arts and young professionals in the past ~15 years. The area is known for its restruant, bars, coffeshops, and nightlife. Fishtown is quickly getting gentrified pushing out long time locals as well as the origional young artists who origioanlly started the gentrification 10-15 years ago.

Queen Village

Queen Village located directly south of Old City, is a quaint residential neighborhood. It has bueatiful housing mainly from the turn of the century and many small coffeeshops, restuarnts, and places to grab a drink. Additionally, the area encompasses part of Philly's famous South Street, which used to be known for it's nightlife. Queen Village offers a great place to live for those looking to settle down.
